Additional Rent: Tenant anticipates that this lease will be a triple net lease and subject to tenants proportionate share of Common Area Maintenance, and that the GLA of the center (including the footage for all anchor tenants) shall be included in the calculation of this proportionate share of Taxes and Insurance. Landlord currently estimates these charges (inclusive of real estate taxes, insurance and common area maintenance fees) at: Landlord shall reconcile costs annually based on a calendar year and adjust the next year’s charges accordingly. In no event shall the costs associated with the common area exceed five (5%) percent of the prior year’s reconciliation; nor shall the administrative or managements fees combined exceed ten (10%) percent of the common area maintenance fees of the prior year’s reconciliation.
Option to Renew: Tenant shall have the right to extend for two (2) periods of three (3) years under the same terms and conditions as outlined in the initial term.
Use/Exclusivity: The premises shall be used for the operation of a Dollar store, and shall carry products typically found in a Dollar or Dollar-Plus-type retail store, including food, candy, and snacks. The Tenant shall be the only Dollar or Dollar Plus-type store in the shopping center. The Tenant shall have the option to sell goods at all price points with the majority of products priced at $1.00. The Landlord warrants that the premises are properly zoned to accommodate this use.
Lease Term Commencement: Commencement of lease term shall be sixty (30) thirty days following Landlord's tender of the premises in “vanilla shell” condition for tenants fixture allowance period.
Rent Commencement: Commencement of the Monthly Rent and additional rent shall be one-hundred twenty (120) days following Lease Term Commencement.
Condition of Premises: Landlord shall deliver to Tenant a “vanilla” shell painted with the Tenant’s specified color as specifically defined in the improvement description attached hereto in Exhibit “A”, including replacing floor tile, Installing drop ceiling with lighting as outlined in Addendum “A” attached and building wall to separate office/storage area. Further, Landlord warrants that upon delivery to tenant the Lease space shall be in conformance with all city, county and state codes, relating to and required for the Tenant’s intended Use.
Tenant Allowance: As an inducement to consummate a lease agreement, The Landlord shall, on the day of Store opening, pay to the Tenant a one-time allowance of $2,000.00 to offset Various costs the Tenant has incurred in conjunction with the opening of this store.
Security Deposit" Tenant will pay a security deposit in the amount of one month’s rent.
Signage: Tenant shall be permitted to install a box-type outdoor sign with a Plexiglas cover, including its corporate logo, neon storefront signage, and maximum pylon signage conforming to all applicable codes and criteria. Landlord to remove existing signage and provide sign criteria when final LOI is submitted.
Advertising: During the first 45 days following the grand opening of the location, tenant shall have the right to install a 4 x 8 banner and/or an ‘A’ frame type sign at the main entrance(s) to celebrate and support the new store opening. Additionally, tenant may place an employee(s) in the entrance area(s) to highlight the grand opening, and for a maximum of 48 hours have the option of displaying items in the common area i.e. sidewalk, and/or a portion of the parking lot for grand opening activities.
HVAC: Landlord will warrant HVAC for the first (12) months, and then lessee’s responsibility shall not exceed $300 per repair.
Relocation: Tenant does not agree to any relocation within the shopping center.
Anchor Co-tenancy: In the event that Publix or any subsequent anchor tenant vacates their location in this center, or ceases to conduct retail business in this location, at any time during the original or option terms of this lease, Tenant shall have the option to terminate this lease without penalty by providing 60 days notice to Landlord. Landlord to provide to Tenant the end dates of leases for the anchor tenant(s).
Non-Disturbance: Landlord acknowledges and agrees it is current with all financial obligations associated with the shopping center including, but not limited to mortgage payments, taxes and NNN items. Further, Landlord guarantees that it has not received a default notice from any lender with any financial interest in the property and has no material knowledge of any impending default. In addition, landlord warrants that the anchor tenant within center has shown no signs of distress and that Landlord has no prior knowledge of impending default.
Drawings of Premises: Landlord shall provide Tenant with most current architectural plans(s) and “as-built” drawing(s) of space.
Gross Income Projection: Should tenant not achieve gross sales of $232,000.00 in the second 12 month period of the initial term of the lease, then the tenant shall have the right to terminate this lease with out penalty with a 90 day notice to the landlord.
Commission: Curry Real Estate Services is to be the Exclusive Tenant Representative of record in this Transaction and shall be paid agreed commission on a separate agreement.

The Leases shall be fully executed within two (2) days after the tenant signs the Lease. Tenant has the right to revoke the lease and it will become null and void if not fully executed, by the date above.

Addition to Letters of Intent:

The lease shall stipulate that Dollar Store Services (or appropriate development company) shall be the development company for the tenant's retail store. (This requirement is intended to give the landlord assurance that the store that will ultimately be opened by the tenant will be of the standard and presentation that they anticipate, and that by being trained and supported after opening, the new store owner will be best positioned to be successful.)

This letter is intended to be non-binding. It is an interim expression of some of the major business terms. This proposal is an effort to outline some of the terms that may be included in a possible lease agreement. There will be no binding agreement or obligation between the Landlord, proposed Tenant or Broker until both Landlord and Tenant have executed and delivered a definitive, binding, written lease agreement. There shall be no burden or obligation upon Tenant or Broker to proceed derived from this letter of terms. As no obligation is created here from, Tenant is examining its options and alternatives. The proposal is subject to revision or withdrawal at anytime for any reason in Tenant’s sole judgment.

Please respond to this proposal on or before April 18, 2013 (“Deadline”)
